In Memory of Strider

2004 - 2013
This boy was sure of his job: helping people. This could mean carrying buckets and tarps while we did yard work, or carrying in the mail, groceries, or whatever you would give him (he loved to carry things). He was my shadow all the time and you never had to ask for his help. He would see what you were doing and just figure out what he could do to help. This willingness to please is why he had so many titles. I would love to say it was my great training (though I did learn with him), but it was really because, for the most part, he could read my mind and just did the exercises once I asked. He had the ideal working newf personality.

Ch. B.I.G.'s Taking It All In Stride, RN, DD, CD, WD (Strider)

In Memory of Onyx

1999- 2010
We lived with Onyx for eleven years. Her only official title was Draft Dog, but she taught us many valuable things. She was sure it was her job to watch over our girls as well as any child that set foot in our yard. She was a wonderful mother who produced eight beautiful puppies. She taught us what it is like to be loved by a Newfoundland and we are forever changed because of her.

HickoryRidge's Onyx (Onyx)
